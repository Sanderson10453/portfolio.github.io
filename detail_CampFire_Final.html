Camp Fire Broadband Analysis

I analyzed MLab download and upload speeds in Butte County, California to understand how residents' internet experience differed from FCC records.

Using open-source, public data to highlight disparities in FCC and user data

During the pandemic, the FCC spent considerable amount of money to close the digital divide — the growing resource gap between those who have access to the internet (who tend to be wealthier, younger, and live in more urban area) and those who do not have access (who tend to be older, less wealthy, and live in rural areas). Unfortunately for home internet users, the FCC relied on Internet Service Provider (ISP) data to inform where they invest in broadband infrastructure. This ISP data is often quite different than what home internet users truly experience.

Process

The first photo shows a visualization of download speeds before and after the Camp Fire — areas that are red have slower download speeds while areas in green have faster speeds. For this analysis, I downloaded MLab speed test data from Google Big Query — the second image shows one of the queries. Once I pulled the data, I analyzed the download data in Stata, a statistical package, to clean the data of noise (Image 3). Finally, I created the map show in the first image to understand what users in Butte County experienced before and after the Camp Fire
