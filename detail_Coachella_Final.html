From the Ground Up

This report shows the statistically significant impact of affordable housing investment on local mortgage values, and qualitative analyses reveal how those investments, combined with intentional investment in civic and physical infrastructure, led to local stability and revitalization, the growth of a middle class, and even changes in political representation.

Investments in affordable housing can support the growth of a middle class

In this report, we sought out to answer the question "What are the impacts of community development efforts on rural communities, and how can these efforts advance equity over the longer term?" As the lead analyst for the San Joaquin Valley, it was my job to understand how investments in housing and infrastructure led to improvements in housing mortgage values.

Process

To understand how investments in housing affected the San Joaquin Valley, an Adjusted Interrupted Time Series (AITS) analysis felt most appropriate. This meant, my first task was to decide what an appropriate study area would be. This is because, while this methodology can show time-delayed effects of events like housing investments, it is very dependent upon the study area and time window. Given this, I explored the tracts where housing investments were made between 1993-2016 - this approach allowed me to look see how residents in the same locale as the investments were made were affected. An example of this exploratory work can be seen in Image 2.

After picking an appropriate study area, I created indicator variables for our various census tracts in order to run the AITS model. This can be seen in Image 3. Finally, I ran the AITS model in Stata with results pointing to affordable housing not decreasing mortgage values. This can be seen in the Image 4 - the chart depicts a very similar pattern for mortgages in our study area against Kern County.
