COVID-19 Small Business Grant Program - Los Angeles County

I designed the small business intake form that would allow small businesses to apply for a grant. Then, I created the protocol and selected businesses to receive small business grants while imbuing equity in the selection process

Using equitable data practices to support small businesses when they need it most

During the COVID-19 pandemic, LISC National and local offices receives millions of dollars to help small businesses across the nation. As a new initative, we lacked the proper infrastructure to select and distribute small business grants at the necessary scale. In Los Angeles county, I designed the small business intake form that would allow small businesses to apply for a grant. Then, I created the protocol to randomly select businesses to receive small business grants while imbuing equity in the selection process

Process

To disperse over $100 million in grants to small businesses, we created multiple rounds that represented multiple opportunities for small businesses to receive funding. In the first image, we can see the number of applicants and general location of the finalists for each LA city district. In order to have applicants, and finalists eventually, I had to design an intake form that was easy to use and collected the necessary information. Using FormsAssembly, I created the intake form that met these needs - this can be seen in Image 2. After applicants have filled out the intake form and a round closes, it was my job to select finalists - to be fair, but ensure equity, I weighted applications and randomly selected applicants who were in the top percentile. In image 3, some of this process is shown through Stata code.
